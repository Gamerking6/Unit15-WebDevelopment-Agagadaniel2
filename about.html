About us

Built to make electric feel familiar

DriveSpace launched with one belief: switching to electric or hybrid shouldn't require becoming an expert first.

Our story

DriveSpace opened in Manchester to solve a specific problem: most car-buying websites are built for people who already know exactly what they want. We built ours for the other 80% — people switching to electric or hybrid for the first time, who have sensible, practical questions and want straight answers.

Every tool on this site exists because a real test-drive customer asked for it: a way to filter by what they actually need, a configurator that's honest about cost, a comparison table that doesn't bury the numbers, and a finance estimate before they commit to a showroom visit.

What we sell

We specialise in fully electric and plug-in hybrid vehicles only, across six models — from compact city cars to seven-seat family SUVs. We don't sell petrol or diesel-only vehicles, which means every member of staff genuinely knows the range in detail.

Accessibility on this site

We've built this site to be usable regardless of how you browse the web. Specifically:

  • All pages can be navigated using a keyboard alone, with a visible focus indicator on every interactive element.
  • A “skip to main content” link is available at the top of every page for screen reader and keyboard users.
  • All images, including vehicle illustrations, have descriptive alternative text.
  • Colour is never the only way information is conveyed — for example, the best value in our comparison table is marked with a symbol as well as colour.
  • Text maintains a minimum contrast ratio against its background in line with WCAG 2.2 AA guidance.
  • The site respects your device's reduced motion setting, removing animation where requested.
  • Forms include clear labels, grouped related fields, and descriptive error messages rather than just colour changes.

If you find anything on this site that's difficult to use, please let us know using the contact details below — we'd genuinely like to fix it.
